Interest Information

Definitions:

Policy Rates are the key interest rates set by a central bank to influence a nation’s economy through monetary policy. It serves as a benchmark that affects other interest rates, such as those for mortgages and savings accounts, thereby managing inflation, credit availability, and economic activity.

Government Yield Bonds are the returns on a government debt security, influenced by market rates and economic expectations. Policy rates do affect these bonds because these are the baselines for the other interest rates. This represents the interest rate you get for lending money.

Formulas:

(Rates must converted into decimals) (Example: 2% = 0.02)
Simple Interest Formula is I = Prt
I = Interest
P = Initial Value
r = Policy Rate
t = time in words

Compound Interest Formula is A = P(1 + (r / n))^nt
A = Future Value
P = Initial Value
r = Policy Rate
n = number of times compounded in “t”
t = time

Continuously Compounded Interest is A = Pe^rt
A = Future Value
P = Interest Value
r = Policy Rate
t = time in years
